What to Shop From Part II of the Yeezy Gap Engineered

Not interested in digging through large trash bags? Shop Ye and Balenciaga creative director, Demna Gvasalia's, latest collaboration online.

A little more than three months ago, Ye and Demna teased us by releasing eight Yeezy Gap engineered by Balenciaga looks. Today, all 25 ensembles, which celebrate Ye and Demna's utilitarian approach to fashion along with Gap's signature take on timeless American style, are available for purchase.

The highly-anticipated lineup reimagines wardrobe essentials, with denim jackets and hoodies elevated with sharp, runway-worthy shoulders and couture-like voluminous proportions. On the opposite side of the spectrum, you'll find body-hugging jumpsuits plus terrifyingly chic face-obscuring masks. It won't be hard to mix and match pieces into your wardrobe, especially since the majority of the offerings are in shades of black, with the exception of a few deep beige and light wash denim items.

When the news was first announced in January, Ye enthusiastically told Vogue, “It is a vision come true to work with Gap and Demna, the creative director of Balenciaga, to make incredible product available to everyone at all times.” We certainly appreciate Ye's efforts to democratise fashion, but we have a feeling this collection will fly off of shelves. But if you're lucky (and move fast), you can shop it on yeezygap.com, and Balenciaga's online retail partners farfetch.com and mytheresa.com before it sells out.

Photo: Courtesy of Yeezy Gap Engineered by Balenciaga

Is there a more fruitful bromance in fashion right now than the one Ye shares with Demna, the creative director of Balenciaga and the mastermind behind the artist's dramatic stylistic transformation? (Whoops, our landline is buzzing: Kim Jones and Eli Russell Linnetz would like a word.) Last week, Ye jetted to the Big Apple to take in Balenciaga's Spring 23 show, held—where else?—on the trading floor of the New York Stock Exchange. Now Demna is returning the favor, once again lending his input to the latest drop from his pal's ongoing partnership with Gap.

First teased in a TV spot aired during an episode of Family Guy (and later postponed in the aftermath of the mass shooting in Texas), the collection, formally billed as “Yeezy Gap Engineered By Balenciaga”, arrives as demand for the latter brand reaches a fever pitch.

This is the second time Ye's tapped the Balenciaga head honcho for help on his Gap project, and it marks the culmination of his longstanding relationship with the similarly mononymous designer, who shaped the look and feel of his buddy's elaborate Donda performances—and who's moody, hulking silhouettes Ye has been stomping around in for a year straight.

Photographs courtesy YEEZY, Getty Images; Collage by Gabe Conte

As far as aesthetic statements go, it's a fairly dramatic one. Gone are the dusty hues that once defined Yeezy's earlier designs, and in their place are shades of washed black, gray, navy, and green, along with a matching denim set in stonewashed blue. The 36-piece capsule is the most comprehensive release yet from the eagerly-anticipated tie-up, and skews eerily close to Ye's own uniform as of late, with plenty of signature Demna-isms thrown in for good measure. (Full-length bodycon suits, anyone?)

But what should you actually buy? That depends. Looking for a freaky riff on Gap's “TIMELESS AMERICAN ICONS”, per the press release's consistently hilarious all-caps parlance? (The double-layer logo hoodie and swishy jersey sweatpants are where we'd start.) Itching to dabble in full-on Ye cosplay? (The padded denim jacket and coordinating distressed jeans are calling your name.) In the mix are also boxy washed anoraks, brawny canvas cargo pants, and plenty of the duo's brisky-selling hardy basics. No matter what you're feeling most, prepare yourself accordingly: If prior items are any indication, sizing is bound to be a crapshoot. Best to make sure you've got your measurements down pat before impulse-buying the whole lot.
